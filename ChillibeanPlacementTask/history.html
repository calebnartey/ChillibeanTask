F1 History

F1 has undergone many changes as cars have seen vast improvements over the years

F1 Origins

F1 originated with the European Championship of Grand Prix motor racing of the 1920s and 1930s. Several races had been completed in the 1940s and the world championship was meant to start before World War II but due to WWII, the championship was not formalised until 1947. The first championship took place in Silverstone, England in 1950. Formula 1 was offically established on May 13, 1950, where from that point, the competition only grew, adding more races per season and expanding to new countries.

F1 1950 - Present

F1 has changed drastically since its opening and the competition has grown exponentially. Engineers have become more intelligent finding different ways to improve a F1 car each year. F1 has become a global sport where races take place in Americas, Africa, Asia and Europe. The growth of F1 has been phenomenal and I would love to see how different it looks in 15 years with new fans watching races and the rules and regulations changing every year.
